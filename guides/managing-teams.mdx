Effective team management is crucial for project success. TeamUp provides flexible tools for adding team members, assigning roles, and managing permissions throughout your project lifecycle.

## Team Member Roles

TeamUp uses a three-tier role system to balance collaboration with appropriate access control:

| Role | Permissions | Use Case |
|------|-------------|----------|
| **Owner** | Full project control, delete project, manage all members | Project creator, primary stakeholder |
| **Maintainer** | Approve tasks, manage contributors, update settings | Team leads, project managers |
| **Contributor** | Create tasks, submit for review, view dashboard | Team members, developers, designers |

## Adding Team Members

### Searching for Users

TeamUp's user search helps you find and add team members by email address.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access Team Management">
    Navigate to your project and click **Team** or **Manage Team** to open the team management interface.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Search by Email">
    Enter the team member's email address in the search field. The search will:
    - Match email addresses as you type
    - Show up to 10 matching results
    - Display users alphabetically
    - Only show users with TeamUp accounts

    <Info>
      The search is case-insensitive and matches partial email addresses. For example, searching "john" will find "john@example.com" and "johnny@company.com".
    </Info>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select the User">
    Click on the correct user from the search results. Their profile information will be displayed for confirmation.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Assign Initial Role">
    Choose the appropriate role for this team member:
    - **Contributor** for most team members
    - **Maintainer** for team leads who will approve work

    <Tip>
      Start with the Contributor role and promote to Maintainer as needed. It's easier to grant additional permissions than to revoke them.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add to Project">
    Click **Add to Project** to send the invitation. The user will immediately see the project in their dashboard and receive a notification.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Inviting Multiple Members

You can add multiple team members in succession:

1. Add the first member following the steps above
2. The search field will clear automatically
3. Search for and add the next member
4. Repeat as needed

<Note>
  All team members are automatically added as contributors by default. The project owner is included in both the owner and maintainer lists.
</Note>

## Managing Roles

### Assigning Roles

Roles define what team members can do within your project. Understanding these roles helps you structure your team effectively.

#### Owner Role
- **Automatically assigned** to the project creator
- **Cannot be transferred** to another user
- **Full permissions** including:
  - Deleting the project
  - Managing all team members
  - Approving all tasks
  - Updating project settings

#### Maintainer Role

Maintainers have elevated permissions for project management:

```text
Permissions:
✓ Approve submitted tasks
✓ Add new contributors to the project
✓ Remove contributors from the project
✓ Update contributor roles
✓ View all project statistics
✗ Delete the project
✗ Remove the owner
```

**When to Assign:**
- Team leads who review work
- Senior developers who validate code
- Project managers coordinating tasks

<Warning>
  Maintainers can remove other maintainers from the project. Choose maintainers you trust with project management responsibilities.
</Warning>

#### Contributor Role

Contributors are your primary team members:

```text
Permissions:
✓ Create new tasks
✓ View assigned tasks
✓ Upload task attachments
✓ Submit tasks for review
✓ View project dashboard
✓ View project statistics
✗ Approve tasks
✗ Add or remove team members
✗ Delete the project
```

**When to Assign:**
- Developers working on features
- Designers creating assets
- Any team member doing hands-on work

### Updating Member Roles

You can change a team member's role at any time:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Team Management">
    Navigate to your project's team management page.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Find the Team Member">
    Locate the member whose role you want to change in the team list.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Change Role">
    Click on their current role and select the new role from the dropdown:
    - **Promote to Maintainer** - Give task approval permissions
    - **Demote to Contributor** - Remove management permissions

    <Info>
      Role changes take effect immediately. The team member will see updated permissions right away.
    </Info>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Confirm Change">
    Click **Update Role** to save the change. The team member will be notified of their new permissions.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Removing Team Members

### When to Remove Members

- Team member leaving the organization
- Project scope changes and member no longer needed
- Member transferred to different project

### Removal Process

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access Team Management">
    Open the team management interface for your project.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select Member to Remove">
    Find the team member you want to remove and click the **Remove** button next to their name.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review Impact">
    Before removing, understand what happens to their tasks:

    <AccordionGroup>
      <Accordion title="Tasks with Single Assignee">
        If the member was the only person assigned to a task, that task will be **permanently deleted**. Make sure to reassign important tasks before removing the member.
      </Accordion>

      <Accordion title="Tasks with Multiple Assignees">
        If the task has other assignees, the member will simply be removed from the assignee list. The task continues normally with remaining assignees.
      </Accordion>

      <Accordion title="Task Approvals">
        If the removed member was a maintainer who approved tasks, their approvals remain valid. The approval history is preserved.
      </Accordion>
    </AccordionGroup>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Confirm Removal">
    Click **Confirm** to remove the team member. This action:
    - Removes them from the project team
    - Removes the project from their dashboard
    - Deletes or unassigns their tasks (as described above)
    - Cannot be undone (you'll need to re-add them)
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  **Data Loss Risk:** Tasks assigned only to the removed member will be permanently deleted. Always reassign or back up important tasks before removing team members.
</Warning>

### What Happens When Members Leave

When a team member is removed from a project:

#### For the Member
- ✓ Removed from project team lists
- ✓ Project disappears from their dashboard
- ✓ Loses access to project tasks and data
- ✓ Cannot view project statistics or dashboard

#### For Tasks
- **Solo-assigned tasks:** Deleted permanently
- **Multi-assigned tasks:** Member removed from assignee list
- **Submitted tasks:** Remain in submission queue
- **Approved tasks:** Approval history preserved

#### For the Project
- ✓ Team count decreases
- ✓ Statistics recalculated without member's contributions
- ✓ Remaining team members unaffected

### Special Cases

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Removing a Maintainer">
    When removing a maintainer:
    - If they're the last maintainer, the owner is automatically added as a maintainer
    - Their previous task approvals remain valid
    - They can be re-added later as a contributor
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Cannot Remove Owner">
    The project owner cannot be removed from the project. If the owner needs to leave:
    - They must delete the entire project, or
    - Transfer all assets to a new project with a new owner
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Member Requests to Leave">
    Members can request to leave a project. This follows the same process as removal, with the same effects on their tasks.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Team Management Best Practices

### Start Small, Scale Smart
- Begin with core team members
- Add specialists as needed
- Keep communication overhead low

### Clear Role Assignment
- Assign maintainers who can review work regularly
- Make role expectations clear in project description
- Document who to contact for approvals

### Regular Team Audits
- Review team roster monthly
- Remove inactive members
- Adjust roles based on contribution patterns

### Before Removing Members

<Checklist>
  - [ ] Identify all tasks assigned to the member
  - [ ] Reassign important tasks to other team members
  - [ ] Document any in-progress work
  - [ ] Notify the team about the change
  - [ ] Update project documentation if needed
</Checklist>

### Communication
- Notify team members before changing their roles
- Explain the reason for role changes
- Provide guidance for new maintainers

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Added wrong person to project">
    Simply remove them from the team using the removal process. If they haven't been assigned any tasks, no data will be affected.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Need to temporarily remove someone">
    TeamUp doesn't have a "suspend" feature. You'll need to remove and re-add the member when they return. Consider reassigning their tasks instead.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Member can't see the project">
    Verify:
    - They're logged in with the correct email address
    - They were successfully added (check team roster)
    - They've refreshed their dashboard
    - No browser cache issues (try incognito mode)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Accidentally deleted tasks by removing member">
    Unfortunately, deleted tasks cannot be recovered. This is why we recommend reassigning tasks before removing members. You'll need to recreate the tasks.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
